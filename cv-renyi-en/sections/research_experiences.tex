\section{Research Experiences}


        \begin{twocolentry}{
            Aug 2025 - Feb 2026
        }
            \textbf{Quantifying the Invisible: Women Doctors in Rosenwald Guides} 
            
            [\href{https://medif.hypotheses.org/2006}{Blog}] [\href{https://renyi.ch/reports/rosenwald.pdf}{Report}] [\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25781}{Preprint}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/double-triangle-annotation}{Annotation}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/extraire-tesseract-openai}{Extraction}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-benchmark}{Benchmark}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-extraction}{Dataset}]
        \end{twocolentry}
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
        \begin{highlights}

    \item Collaborated with historians from the Laboratory for the History of Science and Technology (EPFL) and the Institut des humanit\'es en m\'edecine (Lausanne).
    \item Designed a double-triangular annotation framework combining LLMs and human labeling for scalable and reliable annotation, achieving >50\% reduction in human annotation effort while maintaining >99\% accuracy. \textbf{Preprint: \href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25781}{arXiv:2605.25781}}.
    \item Built a benchmark dataset of 2,600+ doctor entries from the Rosenwald Guides using the proposed framework to support evaluation of structured information extraction from historical documents.
    \item Compared four extraction paradigms on the benchmark and applied the best-performing approach (image + OCR text input) to extract 577,000+ doctor and health official records from the Rosenwald Guides (1887--1922).
    \item Discovered 3,700+ records of female doctors (1887--1922), enabling future study of women's medical practice.
\end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}

            \textbf{On-device medical chatbot for nurse-midwives, \href{https://www.d-tree.org/}{\textit{D-tree}}, Zanzibar, Tanzania} 
            
            [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/mamai}{Code}] [\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29580}{System Preprint}] [\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29467}{Benchmarks Preprint}] [\href{https://nmrenyi-mamai-demo.hf.space}{Live Demo}] [\href{https://youtu.be/M_Kruluel28}{Video Demo}]
        \end{twocolentry}
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
            \begin{highlights}
                \item Built a fully offline, on-device medical RAG assistant---an EmbeddingGemma-300M retriever and a Google Gemma 4 E4B (int4) generator running on commodity Android---that answers nurse-midwives' clinical questions with cited guidance, requiring no connectivity and keeping all patient data on the device
                \item Curated a \href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/mamai-medical-guidelines}{RAG guideline corpus} of 87 authoritative documents (WHO, NICE, MSF, and Zanzibar/Tanzania national guidelines), chunked into 63,650 on-device passages
                \item Designed and ran a layered evaluation of the deployed system, covering end-to-end answer quality, the retriever and the generator each measured in isolation, and on-device latency as a function of input length and CPU/GPU backend
                \item Built an open \href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/mamai-eval}{evaluation harness} over two benchmarks (\href{https://huggingface.co/datasets/nmrenyi/mamabench}{mamabench} and \href{https://huggingface.co/datasets/nmrenyi/mamaretrieval}{mamaretrieval}), scored by an LLM judge validated against physician rubrics from HealthBench
            \end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}
