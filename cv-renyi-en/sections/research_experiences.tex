\section{Research Experiences}


\textbf{Quantifying the Invisible: Women Doctors in Rosenwald Guides (1887–1940) }\hfill  Aug 2025 - Feb 2026  [\href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/12LYws-7jR2z7q76M75Q9VIWCCAzLoBfk/view?usp=share_link}{Report}][\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/double-triangle-annotation}{Annotation}][\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/extraire-tesseract-openai}{Extraction}][\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-benchmark}{Benchmark}][\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-extraction}{Dataset}] 
\begin{highlights}

    \item Collaborating with researchers from the Laboratory for the History of Science and Technology (EPFL) and the Institut des humanit\'es en m\'edecine (Lausanne):
    \item Designed a double-triangular annotation framework combining LLMs and human labeling for scalable and reliable annotation, achieving >50\% reduction in human annotation effort while maintaining >99\% accuracy.
    \item Built a benchmark dataset based on the Rosenwald Guides to support evaluation of structured information extraction from historical documents. 
    \item Quantitatively and qualitatively compared four extraction paradigms on the benchmark and applied the best-performing approach (image + OCR text input) to extract records of doctors and health officials (1887--1922) from the Rosenwald Guides. 
    \item Extracted 3,700+ records of female doctors (1887-1922), enabling future study of women's medical practice.
\end{highlights}
