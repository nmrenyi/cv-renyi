\section{Research Experiences}


        \begin{twocolentry}{
            Aug 2025 - Feb 2026
        }
            \textbf{Quantifying the Invisible: Women Doctors in Rosenwald Guides} 
            
            [\href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/12LYws-7jR2z7q76M75Q9VIWCCAzLoBfk/view?usp=share_link}{Report}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/double-triangle-annotation}{Annotation}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/extraire-tesseract-openai}{Extraction}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-benchmark}{Benchmark}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/rosenwald-extraction}{Dataset}]
        \end{twocolentry}
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
        \begin{highlights}

    \item Collaborated with historians from the Laboratory for the History of Science and Technology (EPFL) and the Institut des humanit\'es en m\'edecine (Lausanne).
    \item Designed a double-triangular annotation framework combining LLMs and human labeling for scalable and reliable annotation, achieving >50\% reduction in human annotation effort while maintaining >99\% accuracy. \textbf{Submitted to ACL Rolling Review (under review)}.
    \item Built a benchmark dataset of 2,600+ doctor entries from the Rosenwald Guides using the proposed framework to support evaluation of structured information extraction from historical documents.
    \item Compared four extraction paradigms on the benchmark and applied the best-performing approach (image + OCR text input) to extract 577,000+ doctor and health official records from the Rosenwald Guides (1887--1922).
    \item Discovered 3,700+ records of female doctors (1887--1922), enabling future study of women's medical practice.
\end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}

            \textbf{On-device medical chatbot for nurse-midwives, \href{https://www.d-tree.org/}{\textit{D-tree}}, Zanzibar, Tanzania} 
            
            [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/mamai}{Code}]  [\href{https://youtu.be/M_Kruluel28}{Demo Video}] [\href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/obgyn-qa-collection}{Dataset}]
        \end{twocolentry}
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
            \begin{highlights}
                \item Deployed \href{https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-3n-E4B-it-litert-preview}{Google Gemma 3n E4B IT (int4)} on edge devices to provide offline medical information for midwives and nurses in low-resource settings (limited Internet connectivity)
                \item Used Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) techniques on official midwife training materials to enhance the system's ability to provide accurate and locally feasible information
                \item Curated an \href{https://github.com/nmrenyi/obgyn-qa-collection}{open OBGYN QA dataset} of 20,500+ questions by filtering five public medical benchmarks (\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.15640}{AfriMed-QA}, \href{https://proceedings.mlr.press/v174/pal22a}{MedMCQA}, \href{https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.10.25.25338798}{Kenya Clinical Vignettes}, \href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.13081}{MedQA-USMLE}, \href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.17028}{Women's Health Benchmark}) using existing tags and Gemini 3 Flash Preview for question classification
                \item Benchmarked the on-device model against leading models (e.g. GPT-5) on the curated OBGYN dataset to evaluate performance gaps and identify areas for improvement
                \item Improving RAG quality and preparing user evaluation studies with nurse-midwives in Zanzibar
            \end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}
